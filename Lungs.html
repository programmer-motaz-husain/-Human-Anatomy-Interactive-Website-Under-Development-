🫁 The Lungs

The lungs 🫁 are essential organs that help us breathe 🌬️
and stay alive ⚡. They are located in the chest 🫀 and are
protected by the rib cage 🦴.

Their main function is to take in oxygen 🫧 from the air
and remove carbon dioxide 💨, which is a waste gas produced
by the body. This process is called gas exchange 🔄.

When we inhale 🌬️, air enters through the nose 👃 or mouth
👄 and travels down into the lungs 🫁. Inside the lungs,
there are millions of tiny air sacs 🫧 called alveoli,
where oxygen passes into the blood 🩸.

At the same time, carbon dioxide 💨 moves from the blood
into the lungs and is removed when we exhale 🌬️.
This keeps the body clean from waste gases 🗑️ and full
of fresh oxygen ⚡.

The lungs also work closely with the diaphragm 💪,
a muscle under the lungs that helps expand and contract
them during breathing 🔄.

This process happens automatically 🤖 without us thinking,
ensuring that every cell in the body receives the oxygen
it needs to produce energy ⚡.
